New Department Capability at OUTFRONT Media

CX Strategy for MBTA RFP Response

The Project

I was asked to create CX strategy that could contribute a competitive edge in a bid for a multimillion-dollar contract with the MBTA.
This was the first time our department was trying this out, as a potential new capability offered by our department.
With no further directions, precedent or formal experience in CX strategy, I stared at the wall for awhile and pondered where to begin. I decided to approach it the same way I do any design problem: by empathizing with the people and perspectives involved – the audiences, businesses and geography – and identify areas of opportunity to create value.

Learning About the People and Perspectives Involved

I held discovery workshops, created surveys, read audience demographic reports, sourced audience segment representatives for 1:1 interviews, researched the business landscape and local geographic points of interest.

User Insights and Areas of Opportunity

The research gave me footing to start building the strategy. For example, one interviewee expressed that a particular line was so slow that he could get to his destination faster by walking. This was reiterated in other research. Since the MBTA wanted the billboard content to nudge the public to start riding again, this was useful perspective in considering messaging that could resonate with the public.

Personas, Journey Maps and Content Strategy

I created detailed personas including research insights that could eventually tie back to the MBTA's objectives, and built journey maps to illustrate each persona's interactions with the billboard screens as they went about their days. Content concepts for the screens in these moments were then crafted through the lens of each persona. I then fleshed out the concepts with consideration to the dynamic capabilities of the screens, such as real-time data feeds or using QR codes for connected experiences. I worked with the team to produce sample creative to help visualize the ideas in the RFP response.

Verdict

After winning the contract, we worked closely with the MBTA to execute on the CX strategy. I was asked to continue my work in this area, producing CX strategy that helped win another multimillion-dollar contract with the CTA and research that was included as part of a marketing study published by OUTFRONT Media.
