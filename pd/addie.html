A Breakdown of the ADDIE Design Model

20 Aug 2015

Instructional design is a systematic process consisting of determining the state and needs of the learner, defining the end goal of instruction, and creating some "intervention" to assist in the transition. It is creating “instructional experiences which make the acquisition of knowledge and skill more efficient, effective, and appealing.” [1] Following this model helps insure that all aspects of the instruction are considered from determining the needs of the learner to the effectiveness of the delivery.

1 Merrill, M. D.; Drake, L.; Lacy, M. J.; Pratt, J. (1996). "Reclaiming instructional design" (PDF). Educational Technology 36 (5): 5–7
