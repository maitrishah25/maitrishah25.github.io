Psychiatry Services

I designed and built a website promoting the services of a psychiatrist who was looking to express her story and expand her private practice, which includes a clientele ranging from fashion models to SNL actors. She wanted the website to liken her services to taking a roadtrip.

Framing the Problem

The client has an existing client base, but has no means by which to educate potential clients about how she could help them. My first task was identifying terms and concepts that are well-known in the field but wouldn't be transparent to potential clients immediately. These boiled down to psychotherapy, psychopharmacology and psychoanalysis.

At the same time, I had to think through the outcome we wanted from the user. Was it for the user to gain expertise in psychiatry? Not really. Ultimately, we wanted the user to make the connection between the client's services and their needs, and to contact the client to learn more or set up an appointment.

Concept and Approach

We collaboratively envisioned that inviting the user on an informal exploration with the client as a cotraveller would take the complex concepts of psychiatry and deliver them as relatable, inviting and beneficial experience.
